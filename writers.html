Alessandro Manzoni

Who was Alessandro Manzoni?


Alessandro Manzoni was an Italian poet, novelist and philosopher. He is well known for the novel I promessi sposi, which is considered one of the masterpieces of world literature and a symbol of the Italian resurgence, both for its patriotic message and because it was a fundamental milestone in the development of the modern, unified Italian language.

I promessi sposi (The Betrothed in english) is an Italian historical novel, first published in 1827, in three volumes, and significantly revised and rewritten until the definitive version published between 1840 and 1842. It is one of the most famous and widely read novel in the Italian language.

Manzoni wrote the first version, Fermo e Lucia, between 1821 and 1823, then he revised it heavily finishing in 1825 which was published in 1827, after two years of corrections and proof-checking. In the early 19th century, Manzoni revised the novel's language for its republication in 1842, cleaning it of many Lombard regionalisms using the dialect of Florence.

Juan Nicasio Gallego

Who was Juan Nicasio Gallego?


Juan Nicasio Gallego was a notable Spanish priest, intellectual, in particular a poet, and public figure with liberal Catholic ideals. He had strong connections to literary and religious circles in Spain and was known for his rhetorical style and moral seriousness.
He became a priest in 1804, then a royal chaplain in Madrid in 1805 and spiritual director of the Royal Palace pages. In Madrid, he got in touch with pre-Romantic intellectuals and began publishing poetry in the Memorial Literario. During the uprising of May 1808 he wrote Influencia del entusiasmo público en las artes.

He moved to Seville and then Cádiz and he became a constituent deputy for Zamora and worked at the Cortes of Cádiz, contributing to the press freedom law. Persecuted by Ferdinand VII for his liberal ideas, he was imprisoned and later exiled for four years. Freed in 1820, he held various ecclesiastical and literary positions.

He admired I Promessi Sposi and considered it as both a religious and civic model, aligning with his worldview. He translated Manzoni's The Betrothed into Spanish and was a member of the Royal Spanish Academy.
